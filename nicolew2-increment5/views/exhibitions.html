Exhibitions

Hello Kitty & Friends: 50 Years of Kawaii

Since her debut on a small vinyl coin purse on November 1, 1974, Hello Kitty has grown into one of the most recognized characters on the planet. This exhibition traces five decades of her evolution through original design sketches, rare vintage merchandise, and immersive displays exploring how her look and meaning have shifted across generations. From early 1970s stationery sets to high-fashion collaborations and contemporary art commissions, visitors can follow Hello Kitty's journey from a simple ribbon-adorned cat into a global icon of friendship, joy, and self-expression. Interactive photo spots and a curated archive of fan-made tributes round out the experience, celebrating the personal connection between Hello Kitty and the millions of fans who have kept her close throughout their lives.

The Kawaii Code: Designing Sanrio's Universe

Behind every Cinnamoroll plush, every My Melody ribbon, and every Kuromi charm is a team of designers who have spent over six decades perfecting the art of cute. This exhibition pulls back the curtain on Sanrio's creative process, presenting original concept sketches, character development timelines, and never-before-seen design prototypes from across Sanrio's roster of more than 450 characters. Through archival displays and hands-on sketching stations, visitors can explore how Sanrio translates warmth, personality, and cultural moments into the simple shapes and soft palettes that have resonated with audiences worldwide. The exhibition also highlights how characters like Pompompurin, Little Twin Stars, and Gudetama each carry a distinct personality and following — showing that the Sanrio universe is far bigger, and far more intentional, than it might first appear.
